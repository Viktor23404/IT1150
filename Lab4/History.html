The first settlement at what is now Prague has been traced to the second half of the 9th century. The oldest building was Vyšehrad, set on a commanding right-bank hill.
For thousands of years that portion of the Vltava’s course where Prague was to rise was crossed by trade routes linking northern and southern Europe.
By the 14th century Prague had become a major central European city, with the Czech money minted at nearby Kutná Hora serving as the hard currency of the entire region.
